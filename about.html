Reliable data is essential for accurate forecasting. Here is an overview of the service we use to power IndyWeather Hub.

Our Primary Data Source

The current conditions and forecasts you see on the IndyWeather Hub homepage are provided by the **Open-Meteo API**.

An **API (Application Programming Interface)** is essentially a messenger that allows our website to request and receive up-to-the-minute weather information from a remote weather server.

This service handles the complex task of gathering meteorological measurements, freeing us to focus on presenting the information clearly and quickly for the Indianapolis area.

About Open-Meteo

Open-Meteo is a service that provides **free access to open-source weather forecast models** from major governmental and research institutions worldwide.

  • **Source of Forecasts:** Open-Meteo collects data from high-quality, publicly available prediction models, such as those run by government weather agencies.
  • **Reliability:** By relying on these authoritative sources, Open-Meteo ensures the underlying forecast data is scientifically sound and frequently updated.
  • **Global Scope:** While we focus on Indianapolis, Open-Meteo aggregates weather information for any location around the world.

When our website loads, it simply asks the Open-Meteo API, "What is the current temperature, wind speed, and weather condition for the specific coordinates of Indianapolis?" Open-Meteo answers instantly, and we translate that into the display you see on the home page.

Quick Summary of the Data Flow

  1. **Request:** Your web browser sends a request to the Open-Meteo server.
  2. **Collection:** Open-Meteo compiles the latest forecast data for Indianapolis.
  3. **Delivery:** The server sends the raw weather numbers back to your browser.
  4. **Display:** Our website code quickly turns those numbers into easy-to-read text, temperatures in Fahrenheit, and helpful weather icons.
